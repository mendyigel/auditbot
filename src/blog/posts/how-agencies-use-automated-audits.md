# How Agencies Use Automated Audits to Win and Retain Clients

Every agency owner knows the feeling: a prospect asks "can you do an audit of our site?" and you have to decide — do you spend 3 hours running the audit for free hoping to win the business, or do you quote them $500 and risk losing them to someone who'll do it for less?

There's a better answer.

## The Agency Problem

Manual website audits are time-intensive. A thorough SEO + performance + accessibility audit across a 50-page site can take 4–8 hours. At $100/hour, that's $400–$800 of unrecoverable time on a proposal that may never close.

The economics don't work unless you win almost every pitch — and no one does.

The result? Most agencies either:
- Skim the audit and deliver a shallow report that doesn't impress
- Charge for the audit and lose prospects who don't want to pay before they're a client
- Skip the audit entirely and lose to competitors who offer one

## The Automated Audit Approach

Agencies that have adopted automated audit tools operate differently. Here's the workflow:

**Step 1: Discovery call.** While still on the call with the prospect, run an audit of their site. In 30 seconds, you have a detailed report covering their biggest SEO, performance, and accessibility issues.

**Step 2: Instant value delivery.** Share your screen or send the report link. You're already showing them problems they didn't know they had, backed by data — not opinion.

**Step 3: Scope the engagement.** The audit becomes the foundation of your proposal. Every issue you found is a line item. You're not guessing at what needs fixing; you're quoting to fix specific, identified problems.

**Step 4: Close faster.** Prospects who see their own broken website in a professional report are far more likely to sign. You've already demonstrated expertise before invoicing a dollar.

## Using Audit Reports as a Sales Tool

The most effective use of an automated audit is as a **cold outreach tool**.

Before reaching out to a prospect, audit their site. Include 2–3 specific findings in your email:

> "I noticed your site has 3 images without alt text and your LCP is 4.8 seconds — which puts you in the 'Needs Improvement' range for Core Web Vitals. Both of these can impact your rankings. Happy to walk you through what we'd do to fix them."

This is miles more effective than "we do SEO and web development." You've already done the work. You already know what's wrong. You're demonstrating value before asking for anything.

## The Retention Play

The sales angle is obvious. The retention angle is underrated.

Agencies that send quarterly audit reports to existing clients keep them longer. Why?

1. **Proof of value:** The report shows what's improved since you started working together. Every fixed issue is evidence of your impact.
2. **New scope:** The report surfaces new issues — which become new project conversations.
3. **Proactive communication:** Clients feel looked after. You're monitoring their site, not waiting for them to call.

Set a reminder to run audits on your top clients every quarter. Send the report with a short summary of key changes. Bill for the follow-up work.
